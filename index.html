Subhas Desa

BIO: Subhas Desa is a Teaching Professor and Director of the Technology and Information Management (TIM) program in Baskin Engineering at UC Santa Cruz.

He joined the School of Engineering at UC Santa Cruz in 2003 to co-create the Technology and Information Management (TIM) program, substantially expanding the then-existing information technology (aka technology of management [TOM]) track with a management of technology (MOT) track. He created and continues to teach all the core courses in the management of technology (MOT) track of the TIM BS degree program: the 5-unit sequence of courses TIM 172A (Introduction to Management of Technology I: Management, Development, and Commercialization of Technologies and Products) and TIM 172B (Introduction to the Management of Technology II: Supply Chain Management); the 3-unit sequence of project courses, TIM 172P (MOT I Project) and TIM 172Q (MOT II Project); and the 2-unit course, TIM 170 (MOT Seminar). The project courses TIM 172P and 172Q are designed to be a two-quarter interconnected project sequence. In TIM 172P the students work in teams to design, develop, and commercialize a new product, within the context of a large technology company like Apple or Microsoft. In TIM 172Q the students design, develop, and analyze the supply chain for the product developed in 172P.

His primary research interests at UCSC lie in the solution of complex multi-disciplinary problems that require the integration of methods and tools from the following subject matter areas: cognitive science, product design and development; supply chain management; finance; software engineering; computer networks; machine learning; and statistical design of experiments (DOE).

Prior to UCSC, he had a career of extensive research, teaching, consulting, and industrial experience in the following capacities: development engineer at HP-San Diego, post-doctoral research associate in Mechanical Engineering at Stanford, faculty member at Carnegie Mellon and Cornell, principal engineer at SC Solutions (a small R&D start-up), and management consultant at McKinsey, the leading global management consulting company.

His academic research and teaching areas during this period included product design and development, manufacturing, advanced physical modelling, dynamics, control, and robotics.
