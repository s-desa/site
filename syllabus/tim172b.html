TIM 172B MOT II Syllabus

About the Course

TIM 172B MOT II, Supply Chain Management, is the second in a sequence of courses in the management of technology.

The first course, MOT I, focused first on the development of the market for a product and then on the development of the product itself. To these ends, MOT I provides a systematic methodology and corresponding set of methods and analytical tools to address the management, development, and commercialization (MDC) of technology and products in an integrated manner.

MOT II focuses on the design and development of the value and supply-chain network for the product, from suppliers through the manufacturer to end customers. To these ends, MOT II will develop and apply methods and tools for the management, design, optimization, and operation of value and supply-chain networks.

Textbook

Chopra, S., and Meindl, P., Supply Chain Management: Strategy, Planning, and Operations, Prentice Hall, third edition, 2009, or a later edition.

Course Prerequisites

TIM 172A; basic probability and statistics; and a good working knowledge of Excel. We will extensively use Solver, the optimization package included with Excel.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the stages, processes, and flows involved in the strategic design, planning, and operation of enterprise value and supply chains.
  • Develop and apply an integrated framework for designing, planning, and operating a supply chain.
  • Develop and apply qualitative and quantitative methods and tools for the effective and efficient management of supply chains.
  • Gain experience with supply-chain management of high-tech products through case studies.

General Comments

  • This course is interactive. We will actively discuss case studies and homework in class. Therefore, attendance is mandatory.
  • To learn the material, it is extremely important to take notes in the classroom and use them when doing homework and performing project tasks in TIM 172Q.
  • Homework must be turned in on time. This is intended to prevent the “bullwhip effect” (see the textbook chapter on coordination and the section on the Beer Game).
  • If you have any problems related to the course, please inform me immediately so that we can resolve the issue quickly.
