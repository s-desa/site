TIM 80C Syllabus

About the Course

TIM 80C is an introduction to the process, methods, and tools that are useful in creating a new technology enterprise (a.k.a. “Startup”). The lectures will focus on developing five important components of a startup: (1) the product concept, (2) the market, (3) the business strategy, (4) the cash flows, and (5) the business plan for financing the startup.

Homework assignments, a midterm examination, and a final examination will be used to develop understanding and mastery of the course content. Case studies and occasional presentations by guest speakers from actual startups will be used to illustrate and augment the tools developed in the lectures.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the process of creating a startup from idea to initial public offering (IPO).
  • Develop and apply tools for designing the product (P) concept.
  • Assess and size the market (M) for a product, and then develop a business (B) strategy for commercialization of the product concept.
  • Develop and apply financial (F) tools for assessing cash flows in the enterprise.
  • Develop a business plan that integrates product (P), marketing (M), business (B), and cash-flow strategies with the business goals of the startup.
  • Develop a financial plan for funding the growth of the startup.

General Comments

No single textbook covers the diverse set of topics and tools that constitute this course. It is therefore critical that you attend the lectures and take good notes. Class handouts will be provided as appropriate to supplement the key topics covered in the lectures.

This course is interactive. We will be actively discussing the course material and homework problems in class. Therefore, attendance is mandatory.

Textbook

Recommended: Jerry Kaplan, Startup: A Silicon Valley Adventure, Penguin Books, 1996 (paperback).

This book is an extended case study on an actual startup in pen computing.
