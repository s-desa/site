TIM 172A MOT I Syllabus

About the Course

MOT I is the first in a sequence of courses on the management of technology. MOT I focuses on new product/technology design and development, while the second course, MOT II, deals with the end-to-end design of the supply-chain network necessary to distribute the product from the supplier through the manufacturer to the customer.

MOT I will provide students with a systematic methodology and a corresponding set of methods and analytical tools for addressing the management, development, and commercialization (MDC) of technology and products in an integrated manner. This approach enables the cost-effective and rapid development of profitable, high-quality technologies and products.

Course Objectives

  • Develop proficiency in applying a structured problem-solving process to complex multidisciplinary problems that require the integration of engineering and business.
  • Demonstrate knowledge of and develop proficiency in the diverse methods and analytical tools used for the management, development, and commercialization (MDC) of technologies and products.
  • Demonstrate knowledge in applying an integrated MDC framework that binds all the methods and tools developed in the course.
  • Gain real-world experience with the MDC framework and its associated methods and analytical tools through comprehensive case-study analysis.

Student Hours

Students in this class will be expected to work about 15 hours per week on the course material, including 3 hours and 15 minutes in class with the instructor and, on average, 12 hours per week of independent work on homework, textbook readings, and case-study readings.

General Comments

  • No single textbook covers the diverse set of topics and tools that constitute this course. It is critical that you attend lectures and take good notes, as you will be responsible for material covered in lectures that is not included in the textbooks.
  • The course will cover both qualitative and quantitative methods and tools useful in the development and commercialization of technology.
  • This course is very interactive. We will actively discuss case studies and homework in class. Attendance is mandatory; please contact me ahead of time if you must miss a lecture for an important reason.
  • Keep a project notebook, which will be used when grading your term project.
  • If you have any course-related problems, please see me immediately so that we can resolve the issue quickly.
